Tracking impact as a design strategist

The shift

You're no longer tracking studies delivered. You're tracking direction shaped, people moved, and thinking that stuck. The unit of impact is not a research report — it's a decision that went differently, a narrative that changed, a framework that got adopted.

What to capture instead of metrics

Quotes — what did someone say after your session, doc, or conversation? Paste it verbatim.

Decisions — what got decided differently because of your input?

Adoption — what framework, language, or artifact got picked up and used by others?

Reach — who was in the room? How senior? How many functions?

Vehicles that signal seniority

At this level, how you show up matters as much as what you do. A 1:1 advisory conversation that changes a VP's thinking is high-leverage. A framework adopted across three teams is high-leverage. A talk at an industry event builds external credibility. Track these explicitly — they're the signal that you're operating as a strategist, not a practitioner.

Strategist promotion signal

Watch your scale distribution in Measure. Cross-functional, Org-wide, and Company entries should be growing. Also watch your impact types — Shaped direction, Defined strategy, and Shifted narrative are the ones that most clearly separate strategist from senior practitioner.

The 3-layer report

Executive (30 sec): "I shaped three strategic pivots and introduced a framework now used across the org."

Peer (3 min): The specific moments — who, what shifted, what was decided.

Evidence (on request): The doc, the deck, the quote, the Slack thread. Build this library as you go.
